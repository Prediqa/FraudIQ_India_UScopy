Stage 1 · Ingestion & Triage

Every claim that lands is OCR-parsed, fingerprinted against prior submissions, attached to the member record, and scored. Triage decides what goes straight through and what gets pulled for review.

payer stage 1 of 6
How the engine works
De-duplicate

Document hash + line-item fingerprint catches the same UB-04 resubmitted under a new claim ID, and the same CPT billed twice in one stay.

FIQ risk score

A blended score (provider history, claim-vs-peer ratio, document completeness, NCCI code-pair conflicts). Mr. Rosche scored 96/100 on intake.

Completeness gate

Missing consent forms, blank transfusion fields, absent severity scores (SOFA/APACHE) are logged as queries before clinical review even starts.

Stage 4 · Clinical Cross-Match

The detection core. FRAUDIQ reconciles the bill against every clinical artifact — physician notes, nursing notes, vitals, labs, drug charts, investigations and operative notes — and scores medical necessity on both InterQual and MCG. The question is always: does the clinical record justify the action and the charge?

payer stage 4 of 6detection core
How the engine works
Notes ↔ bill

Every billed item must trace to an order or note. Daily CXR/Echo billed without daily orders fail this test (MCG over-utilisation).

Labs ↔ drugs

Drug doses are checked against renal/hepatic labs and electrolytes — Meropenem vs eGFR, MgSO₄ vs serum Mg, DAPT vs platelets.

InterQual & MCG

Each case is scored on InterQual SI/IS gates and MCG care-pathway milestones — the two frameworks are compared head-to-head.

Stage 5 · Coding & Pricing Audit

With the clinical picture settled, FRAUDIQ audits how it was coded and priced: MS-DRG & MCC support, unbundling, NCCI edits, Medically Unlikely Edits (MUE), drug pricing against CMS ASP / NADAC / WAC, and the proportion of the claim sitting in implants, pharmacy and consumables.

payer stage 5 of 6
How the engine works
Bundling & NCCI

Items inside a per-diem or surgical package (piped O₂, daily rounds, monitoring) must not be billed separately.

Pricing ceilings

Drug and device prices are checked against Medicare ASP, NADAC and invoice cost; markup excess is disallowed.

Composition ratio

When implants + pharmacy + consumables dominate the claim, FRAUDIQ samples those lines hardest — that is where padding hides.

Select a provider for its full MA scorecard, claim funnel, turnaround times and HCC/RAF gap analysis. Every claim links back to its clinical documentation and risk-adjustment outcome.
